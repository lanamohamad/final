About Me

Born in 2003, I am a Biomedical Engineering student at the University of Cincinnati going into my 5th year starting in Fall 2026. I have a strong passion for innovation, leadership, and problem solving. I've grown a love for Engineering as well as learned how skilled I could be at leading and teaching others, in which I take great pride in.

Why Engineering? 🧠

Growing up, they always asked me what I wanted to be when I grew up. I always said I wanted to be an inventor. I was quickly told that it wasn't an occupation (which I wasn't sure why). I continued to pursue Biomedical Engineering due to my love for the Medical field as well as my love for math, I thought it would be simple. I quickly learned I was very wrong, but also learned I was pursuing exactly what I always imagined. Though, at the age of 12 I was more interested in inventing computers and cool robots, building medical devices is just as cool I guess.

My Role Model

I spend a lot of my time speaking about my brother who I look up to with great admiration. He is the sole reason I am where I am today, with support from the rest of my family as well. It is rare to have someone care about you so much, and lead you to success. When I was two years old, my brother saved me from a drowning incident, and for that I am forever greatful, for I don't know what would have happened had I stayed in the water any longer. For the rest of my life, my brother took great care of me and paved many paths for me. He ensured I got in a great major and helped me through those first stages of college where it can be so easy to quit.
