Where I am from

I was born in Ohio on October 21st 2003. However, my parents were born in Baghdad, Iraq. My brother was born in Iraq, shortly after came my sister once my parents moved to Jordan, and from there they made the move to America in 2000. Iraq is a quite the subject in America for a long list of reasons, none that I need to touch on. Being Arab has given me a unique idenity in my upbringing that I didn't come to appreciate till I got older, but it introduced a lot of stuggle in my young years. My family is Muslim which also was quite isolating in a country where most people are Christian.I love my religion and I love sharing it with others, even if I had an opposing view when I was little.

Third culture lifestyle

I was always different than my peers. I went to school in Morrow, Ohio, and was the only Muslim or Arab in my grade and neighboring grades. I never could relate to my friends. Easter and Christmas was splotchy for me. My friends parents let them do whatever they wanted, and though my parents were pretty lenient, there were many rules that were non-negotiables. Rules that sounded nuts to the common American.

The culture and the food in my household was very different than my friends, which I think I appreciated. We had a different language (Arabic) that we could use around guests that they wouldn't understand, which came in handy often. Speaking Arabic today is now a cool party trick, but something I would get made fun of when I was younger. I dealt with many people in school, which was filled with countryfolk, telling me to go back to ISIS or that I was a terrorist. Luckily I didn't let it bother me - but the jokes were always there. Whenever 9/11 came around in school, I got many stares. Getting singled out during any history class was also never fun.

Having this third culture feeling where I don't relate with anyone in America, but when I visit places like Jordan, I am so far removed from that culture as well, made me feel like I was in this third section. This makes a lot of things difficult, but it's quite the conversation starter.
